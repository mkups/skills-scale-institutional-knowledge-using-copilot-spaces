# OctoAcme Project Management Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured, lifecycle-based approach to project management grounded in customer-first principles and iterative delivery. The process encompasses five key phases: **Initiation**, where a Project One-pager and stakeholder alignment validate business need and success metrics; **Planning**, where work is broken into prioritized, estimated backlog items with clear acceptance criteria and a documented Definition of Done; **Execution**, managed through daily standups and weekly syncs using a GitHub Projects board with columns from Backlog to Done; **Release**, which includes pre-release checklists, smoke testing, and rollback plans; and **Retrospectives**, where learnings are captured and converted into actionable improvements. This phased approach ensures clarity at each stage and reduces ambiguity through explicit decision gates.

Clear roles definition is central to OctoAcme's success. **Project Managers** coordinate schedules, risks, and communications while maintaining project documentation and status reporting. **Product Managers** define what should be built, prioritize the backlog based on customer value, and measure outcomes through success metrics. **Developers** implement features to acceptance criteria, write tests, and identify technical risks. **QA/Testing** validates quality and acceptance criteria. This distributed ownership model—with each project having a named PM and Product Lead—ensures accountability while enabling cross-functional collaboration.

Communication happens through a regular cadence designed to keep stakeholders informed and blockers surfaced early. Teams conduct **daily standups** (15 min) focused on progress and blockers, **weekly PM and Product Lead syncs**, **twice-weekly delivery team standups**, and **monthly stakeholder updates**. Risks are managed through a Risk Register updated weekly, with escalation levels: team-level triage in standups, PM escalation to Product Lead and dependent teams, and sponsor-level escalation for business-impacting issues. This multi-level escalation ensures issues are resolved at the appropriate level without creating bottlenecks.

Quality and observability are built into execution through automated and manual practices. Teams implement **unit and integration tests**, **end-to-end smoke tests** for critical flows, **security scanning in CI**, and **manual QA for feature acceptance**. Pull requests follow standards of ≤400 lines with issue links and acceptance criteria, requiring at least one approval before merge. Success is tracked through velocity, burndown, and dashboards monitoring key signals like errors, latency, and usage, enabling data-informed decisions throughout the project lifecycle.
